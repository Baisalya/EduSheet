Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 23, 2026

EduSheet is designed as an offline-first app for teachers and educators. The app helps users create question papers, manage saved papers and question banks, generate OMR sheets, scan question text from images, use a calculator, preview supported document files, and convert supported local files. EduSheet does not require an account and does not sell personal data.

Who We Are

This Privacy Policy applies to the EduSheet mobile application, package name com.baishalya.edusheet. In this policy, "EduSheet", "we", "our", and "us" refer to the EduSheet app and its developer.

Privacy contact: support@edusheet.com

Information EduSheet Handles

EduSheet stores and processes most information locally on your device. Depending on the features you use, the app may handle the following information:

  • Question paper content: paper titles, instructions, school names, exam names, section names, questions, options, answers, marks, and formatting choices entered by you.
  • Question bank content: questions, subjects, chapters, difficulty levels, answers, explanations, and favorites created or imported by you.
  • Template data: custom paper templates, layout settings, colors, header fields, and selected logo/image file paths.
  • Images selected by you: camera or gallery images used for OCR, cropping, school logos, template images, or OMR-related workflows.
  • Recognized text: text extracted from selected images using on-device OCR.
  • Document information: file names, file paths, file sizes, modification dates, file contents needed for local previews or conversions, and file types for supported files such as PDF, Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and text files in folders you allow EduSheet to scan.
  • App preferences: theme setting and whether the current privacy policy has been accepted.

How We Use Information

EduSheet uses the information above only to provide app features, including:

  • creating, editing, saving, previewing, printing, and exporting question papers;
  • saving and organizing your local question bank;
  • generating OMR sheets and PDFs;
  • recognizing text from images you choose or capture;
  • showing and previewing supported documents from your device when you open the document reader;
  • converting supported files locally when you use the Word converter or export features;
  • opening files shared to EduSheet from other apps;
  • remembering app preferences such as dark mode and privacy acceptance.

Offline Storage and Device Processing

EduSheet is offline-first. Saved papers, question bank data, custom templates, and app preferences are stored on your device using local app storage. EduSheet does not operate its own cloud server for these features and does not upload your papers, questions, images, documents, or recognized text to an EduSheet server.

If you uninstall the app or clear app data, locally stored EduSheet data may be deleted by your device operating system. Files that you separately export, print, share, or save outside the app are controlled by you and by the apps or services you choose to use.

Permissions and Access

EduSheet asks for permissions only when needed for app features:

  • Storage or all-files access: used to find, preview, convert, and open supported document files from common folders such as Downloads, Documents, EduSheet export folders, and messaging app document folders, and to work with files shared from other apps. EduSheet scans for supported document types and does not use this access for advertising or tracking.
  • Camera: used when you choose to capture an image for OCR or related education workflows.
  • Photos, gallery, or media access: used when you choose an image for OCR, cropping, logos, templates, or OMR-related workflows.

You can deny permissions, but some features may not work without the relevant access. You can also change app permissions in your device settings.

OCR and Google ML Kit

EduSheet uses Google ML Kit Text Recognition for OCR. OCR processing of your selected images is performed on the device. According to Google ML Kit's terms and privacy documentation, ML Kit input data such as images and resulting outputs are not sent to Google servers for processing. ML Kit may contact Google servers for updates, model downloads, bug fixes, compatibility information, and may send diagnostics, performance, utilization, device, app, and related technical metrics to Google.

For more information, see Google ML Kit Terms and Privacy, Google ML Kit Android Data Disclosure, and Google Privacy Policy.

Sharing and Disclosure

EduSheet does not sell your personal data. EduSheet does not share your papers, question bank content, documents, images, or recognized text with third parties except when:

  • you choose to print, export, open, or share a generated file through your device or another app;
  • you open a document using another installed app;
  • third-party SDKs, such as Google ML Kit, process limited technical diagnostics as described above;
  • disclosure is required to comply with applicable law, regulation, legal process, or enforceable governmental request.

Data Retention and Deletion

EduSheet keeps locally saved papers, question bank data, templates, app preferences, and cached incoming documents on your device for as long as the app needs them or until you delete them, clear app data, or uninstall the app. Some temporary files may also be managed and removed by your operating system.

You can delete saved papers and questions from within the app where deletion controls are available. You can remove all locally stored app data by using your device settings to clear EduSheet app data or by uninstalling EduSheet.

Security

EduSheet is designed to minimize data transfer by processing and storing app content locally whenever possible. Where third-party SDKs transmit technical diagnostics, those providers are responsible for their own security practices. Google states that ML Kit diagnostic data listed in its disclosure is encrypted in transit using HTTPS.

No method of storage or transmission is completely secure. Please avoid adding sensitive personal, financial, medical, or confidential information to worksheets or files unless you are comfortable storing that information on your device.

Children's Privacy

EduSheet is intended as a productivity tool for teachers and educators. The app does not require accounts and does not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information through content stored in the app, you can delete that content from the device or contact us for guidance.

International Users

Because EduSheet primarily stores data locally on your device, your app content generally remains on the device you use. Third-party services such as Google ML Kit may process technical diagnostics according to their own policies and infrastructure.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy when app features, permissions, third-party SDKs, or legal requirements change. If changes are material, EduSheet may show an in-app notice or ask you to review the updated policy again. The effective date at the top of this page shows when this policy was last updated.

Contact Us

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or EduSheet's privacy practices, contact us at support@edusheet.com.
